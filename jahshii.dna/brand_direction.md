# 1st Nation eBikes — Brand Direction
**Artist:** Jahshii
**Product:** Electric scooters & bikes
**Brand Name:** 1st Nation eBikes
**Short Name:** 1st Nation

---

## Concept

Ancient consciousness, clean energy. Jahshii's music carries the weight of roots, fire, and a prophet's certainty. 1st Nation eBikes maps that energy onto movement itself — getting through the earth with purpose, leaving less damage than you found. This is not a tech product. It is a philosophy with wheels.

The "first" in 1st Nation is deliberate: first people, first frequency, first to move differently. The brand does not chase trends. It precedes them.

---

## Logo / Wordmark

- **"1st Nation"** — clean, geometric, with real visual weight. The "1" functions as both ordinal and number — the brand is first and it knows it
- **"eBikes"** beneath it, smaller, in the same family but lighter — almost secondary
- A secondary mark: a minimal geometric motif derived from Taíno/indigenous Caribbean visual language — not decorative, structural. Used as a frame emblem and brand stamp
- Earth tones across all applications — no neon, no chrome

## Typography

- **"1st Nation":** Bold, grounded geometric sans — slightly wider than typical, rooted not sharp. Could use Barlow SemiBold, Raleway Bold, or a custom-weighted Outfit
- **"eBikes" / supporting text:** Same family, regular weight, clean
- **Body copy:** Space Grotesk or similar — warm and modern, not tech-cold
- No condensed fonts — this brand is wide, not compressed

---

## Frame Design (Conceptual)

The bikes are sleek urban ebikes and compact scooters. Earth tone colourways as standard. The tribal geometric motif appears as a panel inlay on the frame — raised or laser-etched, not a sticker. Handlebars have a natural material accent (cork or wood grip, where feasible).

| Model | Style | Colourway |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| 1st Nation Roots | Urban ebike, step-through frame | Midnight + Ochre |
| 1st Nation Earth | City scooter, compact | Forest + Linen |
| 1st Nation Ember | Performance ebike, upright frame | Ember + Black |

## AI Image Direction

**Hero shot:** The Roots model parked on a coastal Jamaican road at golden hour. The sun is low and behind the bike, creating a warm rim light on the frame. The ochre accent catches the light. No rider — just the bike, the road, the ocean in the background.

**Rider shot:** Rider moving through hills at sunrise. Motion blur on the background, the bike sharp. Rider not in racing position — upright, relaxed, purposeful. Full shot from the side.

**Detail shot:** Close-up of the tribal geometric motif on the frame panel. Early morning light, slight dew on the metal. The motif feels ancient and precise simultaneously.

**Urban shot:** Bike parked against a weathered Kingston wall with peeling paint and natural texture. The ochre of the accent plays against the warm tones of the wall. Documentary feel.

**Overhead shot:** Bike from directly above on a natural surface — grass, earth, or stone. Symmetrical composition. The motif visible. Clean, almost spiritual.

**Lighting rule:** Natural light, warm bias. Golden hour preferred. No cold studio light. The product should feel like it belongs outdoors, in real light, on real terrain.
